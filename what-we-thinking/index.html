Community Creative Events

So far, my blog's content has mostly been for community blogging events, especially the OSR Secret Santicorn. My first post was a TTRPG adventure I made in three days for last year's Santicorn, and since then I've compiled a history of that event, wrote some more for past blog prompts, participated in Prismaticwastelands' Merry Hexmas 2025, and hosted my own Secret Sanitcorn in 2025. This year, I intend to continue to regularly participate in community blog events and even host my own. I'll be the host of the RPG Blog Carnival in March with the theme "Tiny Epics: Small Souls in a Big World!". Future Prismaticwasteland blogwagons will be calling my name I am sure, and the IndieWeb Blog Carnival looks promising as well. The design of community creative events is very intriguing, so look forward to a design post from my where I consider existing event types and consider new ones!

I find community blog events to be good fun and a great way to connect with the existing blogging community around a hobby. While I will continue these and other community blog events, I do not intend it to be my main form of content.

Why Tabletop Roleplaying Games (TTRPGs)?

A natural question to ask! TTRPGs have been a love of mine since I was young and I recently delved deeper into them as I have found they let me explore a plethora of my hobbies all together!

  • I practice my 2D art, whether traditional sketches or digital painting.
  • I hone my narrative ability in storytelling, improvisation, and story craft.
    • Designing good adventures requires the above in combination with a consideration of the players and their play experience as we work together to tell a story. A cocktail I seem to love!
  • I design & develop TTRPGs faster than video game development, which is another of my hobbies, but one that has a steeper learning curve for the tools.
    • I've slowly been learning Godot
  • Mathematical modeling of probabilities and game theory certainly applies along with designing a good user experience.
  • I also get to explore civilization structure, operations, and politics.

Truly a great creative outlet. Perhaps best of all, I get to have fun with my friends and the lovely community of TTRPG designers!

Some of my favorite things.
